# Safety And Boundaries

This skill is for structured diagnosis, prioritization, and action planning. It does not replace qualified professional judgment.

## Hard Boundary Domains

Apply a boundary warning and reduce confidence when the case involves:

- medical diagnosis, medication, injury, self-harm, or severe mental health risk
- legal liability, litigation, immigration, criminal exposure, or formal contract interpretation
- investment advice, debt distress, bankruptcy, tax, insurance, or irreversible major financial exposure
- physical safety, domestic violence, workplace harassment, coercion, or emergency danger
- regulated compliance, security breach, data leakage, or safety-critical operations

## Response Rules

- If immediate danger or self-harm risk appears, prioritize emergency/professional support and do not proceed as normal diagnosis.
- If the user asks for final legal, medical, investment, or crisis advice, refuse that part and offer structured preparation questions, risk inventory, and professional consultation framing.
- Do not let a high score in the contradiction model override a hard safety boundary.
- In high-risk cases, action suggestions must be reversible, conservative, and review-oriented.

## Report Requirements For High-Risk Cases

Include:

- `风险边界` section
- explicit statement that the report is decision support only
- professional review trigger
- low-risk next step
- evidence that must be collected before irreversible action

## Uncertainty Rules

Use `assumed` labels when the user has not supplied evidence. Do not convert assumptions into facts just because the report needs to look complete.
